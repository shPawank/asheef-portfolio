About Me

I am Asheef Iqubbal, currently working as a Senior Research Associate at CUTS International (CCIER), Jaipur. My work focuses on digital rights, data governance, emerging technologies, and platform regulation. I contribute to evidence-based policy discussions through detailed research and analysis.

Born on 8th July 1994, I hold Indian nationality. I earned my Bachelor of Technology in Mechanical Engineering from Maharshi Dayanand University (2016) and pursued a Master of Arts in Social Exclusion & Inclusive Policy from Jamia Millia Islamia in 2024.

I bring diverse professional experience. Since February 2022, I’ve been at CUTS. Before this, I worked at the Digital Empowerment Foundation (2019–2021) and served as a Ground Correspondent at NewsCentral24x7 (2018–2019).

Some of the key projects I've led or contributed to include: Economic Analysis of Verifiable Parental Consent Mechanisms, Deepfake Advisory Analysis, Multi-Party Privacy in Digital Credit, Ease of Doing Digital Business in India, Status of E-Consumers in India, and Ethical 6G Framework between India and Australia.

My publications address data protection, healthcare data, AI governance, and digital economy. Recent writings include: The ‘Parental Consent’ Problem with DPDP Rules, Standardising Standard Setting for 6G, Dealing with the Menace of Gen-AI Fakes, and The E-Pharmacy Conundrum.

I continue to engage in shaping India’s digital future through collaborative research, writing, and policy dialogue.
